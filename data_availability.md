# Data Availability Statement

**To include in the manuscript (before the References section),  
as required by Taylor & Francis journal policy.**

## Suggested statement for the manuscript

> The code and experimental notebooks used in this study are openly available  
> on GitHub at https://github.com/[YOUR_USERNAME]/mtsk-classifier  
> and archived on Zenodo at https://doi.org/[ZENODO_DOI].  
> The fine-tuned classification model is available on Hugging Face at  
> https://huggingface.co/crojasc2/mtsk-classifier.  
> The annotated dataset of research articles on MTSK cannot be made  
> publicly available due to copyright restrictions on the original publication  
> abstracts; however, it is available upon reasonable request to the  
> corresponding author.

## Notes

- Replace `[YOUR_USERNAME]` with your actual GitHub username before submission.
- Replace `[ZENODO_DOI]` with the DOI assigned by Zenodo after archiving  
  (see the repository README for instructions).
- The Zenodo DOI is the **persistent identifier** required by Taylor & Francis.
- Taylor & Francis journals require a Data Availability Statement (DAS)  
  submitted **within** the manuscript, placed before the References section.

## Policy compliance

This statement complies with the Taylor & Francis  
**"Share upon reasonable request"** data sharing policy  
(https://authorservices.taylorandfrancis.com/data-sharing-policies/share-upon-reasonable-request/).

For open code: the code repository (GitHub + Zenodo) satisfies the  
reproducibility requirements for computational research.

For the dataset: restricted sharing is permitted under the policy when  
ethically or legally justified (e.g., third-party copyright on source texts).
